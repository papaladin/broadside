⚓ Captain’s Handbook

A quick reference for the Caribbean, 1695

Gold

  • Earned through missions, plunder, and trade.
  • Spent on repairs, crew, provisions, equipment, ships.
  • Wages paid daily : 2g per crew member (×1.5 if morale < 30).
  • If you run out of gold, you cannot hire, repair, or buy provisions.

Fame

  • Your permanent reputation across the Caribbean.
  • Gained from completing missions (especially combat).
  • Gates access to better ships, equipment, and hidden ports.
  • Never decays.

Infamy

  • Your criminal notoriety.
  • Gained from smuggling, attacking patrols, and certain missions.
  • At 50 infamy, bribes are blocked.
  • At 100, you become a Legendary Outlaw.

Faction Heat

  • Short‑term alert level for each colonial power (0‑10).
  • Rises when you fight their patrols or ships.
  • Decays by 1 every 2 days at sea.
  • Higher heat → more random patrols at sea.

Hull

  • Your ship’s hit points.
  • Damaged in combat and storms.
  • If it reaches 0, you are defeated.
  • Repair at any port’s Shipyard.

Crew

  • More crew → faster combat actions.
  • Hire at ports with a Crew service.
  • Dismiss or lose crew in battle.
  • Wages paid daily; low morale can double wages.

Morale

  • Affects combat effectiveness, travel speed, and wages.
  • Drops with empty provisions, unpaid wages, or combat losses.
  • Rises with mission success, drinks, and shore leave.
  • Below 50 → travel slows. Below 30 → wages increase. At 0 → crew deserts.

Reputation

  • Per‑port standing (0‑100), shown as a label (At War → Allied).
  • Affects repair cost, mission rewards, and service access.
  • Decays slowly toward 50 over time.
  • Attacking a faction’s ships reduces reputation with all its ports.

Speed & Hold

  • Speed determines how many days a voyage takes.
  • Hold capacity limits how much cargo you can carry.
  • Hold load > 50% slows your ship (+11% travel time).
  • Hold load > 75% slows it further (+33%).

Resources & Trade

  • Food & Water consumed daily (1 unit per 10 crew).
  • Empty provisions → morale loss; after 14 days no food / 3 days no water, crew die.
  • Prices vary per port based on availability and random variance.
  • Buy low in one port, sell high in another for profit.
  • Goods marked Illegal (tobacco, slaves) risk confiscation by patrols.

Combat & Intercept

  • Intercept options: Fight, Flee, Parley, Bribe, Surrender. Navy patrols also offer Allow Inspection.
  • Surrendering costs gold, cargo, days, or reputation depending on context.
  • If contraband is found during inspection, it is seized and you are fined.
  • Combat actions: Broadside (reliable), Precision (risky high damage), Grapple (board and capture), Evade (flee).
  • Boarding (grapple victory) kills some of your crew proportional to resistance, then allows plunder.
  • Patrol missions: enemy encounter is not guaranteed — sail near the target port and advance days; chance increases with voyage progress.

Mission Types

  • Trade – buy goods cheap, deliver for profit.
  • Smuggle – deliver illegal goods; risk patrol inspection.
  • Combat – hunt down a specific enemy ship.
  • Patrol – clear a rival’s waters of hostile vessels.
  • Escort – protect a convoy to its destination.
  • Assault – attack a port’s garrison by force.

Crew Traits

  • Hidden traits (drunkard, coward, greedy) – revealed over time.
  • Scars earned from storms, battles, shipwrecks.
  • Progression: Seasoned (50 days) → Veteran (100 days) → Loyal (200 days).
  • Upset crew may desert if you attack their home faction.
